Developer

Matt decided to tackle tutorials online through Code Year but, craving more information, he pursued more in depth resources. It was that drive to improve that led him to enroll in General Assembly's Web Development Intensive. The following are some of the projects he has completed. Matt is an incessant builder and hopes to update both the functionality of this portfolio and ship many new projects in the years to come.

Dossier

Craving a new challenge, Matt sat down with friend and GA classmate Harrison Powers to cook up Dossier. The idea is to study up on faces before you meet, or even after, to avoid any embarassment if you forget. Everyone loves having their name remembered, so we used authorization with Facebook and LinkedIn to create this useful little app. While you're there, you can also toggle your RSVP's and play some memory games.

Court of Popular Opinion

The court was invented to help people find solutions to their problems by sourcing social media. It features some AJAX touches and OmniAuth authentication allowing the site to send tweets on behalf of the users.

Grand Send Station

Rock climbing is gaining in popularity in New York City, and it has swept up the author of this page with it. This app seeks to help members find each other and communicate. Authentication was made from scratch through Rails and the site features geocoding to help locate other logged in members of the app.
